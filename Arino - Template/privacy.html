1. Scope

This Privacy Policy applies to personal data collected through:

  • our website;
  • contact forms, inquiry forms, and booking or service request forms;
  • email, phone, WhatsApp, and other professional communication channels;
  • meetings, proposals, quotations, contracts, invoices, and project execution stages; and
  • any other interactions related to our services or business activities.

This Privacy Policy is intended to be applied in accordance with the laws and regulations of the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia, including the Personal Data Protection Law (PDPL) and its implementing regulations, to the extent applicable to our activities.

2. Personal Data We Collect

Depending on the nature of your interaction with us and the services requested, we may collect and process the following categories of personal data:

2.1 Identity and Contact Data

This may include:

  • full name;
  • company or organization name;
  • email address;
  • phone number;
  • job title or position; and
  • city or other relevant business contact details.

2.2 Project and Service Data

This may include:

  • inquiry details;
  • requested service type;
  • production, filming, advertising, or event coverage requirements;
  • scheduling preferences and timelines;
  • location details;
  • project references, creative briefs, notes, files, and attachments; and
  • any other information you voluntarily provide in connection with a request or project.

2.3 Contractual and Financial Data

Where relevant, we may collect information necessary for:

  • preparing quotations and proposals;
  • issuing agreements and invoices;
  • processing or following up on payments; and
  • maintaining related business and accounting records.

2.4 Technical and Usage Data

When you use our website, we may collect certain technical information, such as:

  • IP address;
  • browser type and version;
  • device type;
  • operating system;
  • pages visited;
  • time and duration of visits;
  • referring website or source; and
  • cookie-related or analytics-related data.

2.5 Other Information You Voluntarily Provide

If you choose to send us additional information, including through forms, emails, briefs, or project documentation, we may process that information to the extent necessary for the relevant purpose.

3. Purposes of Processing

We may process personal data for legitimate business and operational purposes, including to:

  • respond to inquiries, requests, and communications;
  • prepare quotations, proposals, pricing, and service information;
  • manage, coordinate, and deliver creative, production, advertising, and media services;
  • communicate with clients, vendors, contractors, freelancers, and project stakeholders;
  • prepare contracts, invoices, and related records;
  • improve website performance, functionality, usability, and communication quality;
  • maintain system, operational, and website security;
  • comply with legal, regulatory, tax, accounting, and professional obligations; and
  • establish, exercise, or defend legal rights and legitimate business interests.

4. Legal Basis for Processing

Where required under applicable law, we process personal data on one or more of the following legal bases, as applicable:

  • your consent;
  • the need to take steps at your request before entering into a contract;
  • the performance of a contract or service arrangement;
  • compliance with legal or regulatory obligations; and
  • legitimate interests, where permitted by applicable law and not overridden by your rights.

5. Sources of Personal Data

We generally collect personal data directly from you. In certain cases, we may also receive business-related personal data from:

  • the company or organization you represent;
  • your authorized representatives;
  • project stakeholders acting on your behalf;
  • publicly available lawful sources; or
  • service providers or platforms used in connection with our website or service delivery.

6. Sharing and Disclosure

We do not sell personal data.

We may disclose or share personal data only where necessary, appropriate, and lawful, including with:

  • website hosting providers, IT service providers, communication platforms, and analytics providers;
  • professional advisers, including legal, accounting, administrative, or compliance advisers;
  • vendors, project partners, contractors, freelancers, or collaborators involved in delivering the requested services;
  • competent governmental, regulatory, judicial, or law enforcement authorities where disclosure is required by applicable law, regulation, or legally binding request; and
  • other parties where you have given consent, where such consent is required.

Where we engage third-party service providers or processors, we seek to ensure that they are subject to appropriate confidentiality, security, and data protection obligations.

7. International Data Transfers

Where the use of hosting services, digital platforms, software tools, cloud systems, or service providers involves the transfer of personal data outside the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia, we will take such steps in accordance with applicable legal and regulatory requirements and implement appropriate safeguards where required.

8. Cookies and Analytics

We may use cookies and similar technologies to:

  • operate and support the website;
  • understand usage patterns and measure website performance;
  • improve user experience and website functionality; and
  • support website security and core technical functions.

You may be able to manage or disable certain cookies through your browser settings. However, disabling some cookies may affect the performance or functionality of parts of the website.

9. Data Retention

We retain personal data only for as long as necessary to fulfill the purposes described in this Privacy Policy, including for:

  • managing inquiries and service requests;
  • maintaining project and business records;
  • complying with legal, regulatory, accounting, tax, or contractual obligations; and
  • protecting our legal rights and legitimate interests in the event of claims or disputes.

When personal data is no longer required for the relevant purpose and no legal basis for continued retention applies, we will seek to securely delete, destroy, or anonymize it, as appropriate.

10. Data Security

We implement reasonable and appropriate administrative, technical, and organizational measures designed to protect personal data against unauthorized access, loss, misuse, alteration, destruction, or unlawful disclosure.

These measures may include, where appropriate:

  • access controls;
  • restricted internal access;
  • secure systems and technical safeguards;
  • document and record management procedures; and
  • internal operational and confidentiality controls.

However, no system or method of transmission over the internet or electronic storage can be guaranteed to be completely secure.

11. Your Rights

Subject to applicable laws and regulations in the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia, you may have certain rights regarding your personal data, including the right to:

  • be informed about the legal or practical basis for collecting your personal data and the purpose of such collection;
  • request access to your personal data;
  • request a copy of your personal data in a clear and readable format, where applicable;
  • request correction, completion, or updating of inaccurate or incomplete personal data; and
  • request deletion or destruction of personal data where permitted under applicable law.

To submit a privacy-related request, please contact us at:
info@art-ratio.com

We may request information necessary to verify your identity before responding to your request.

12. Marketing Communications

We may use your contact details to communicate with you regarding:

  • your inquiries;
  • requested services;
  • quotations and proposals;
  • project coordination; and
  • related business communications.

If we send any promotional or marketing communications, we will do so in accordance with applicable legal requirements.

13. Children and Minors

Our website and services are not intentionally directed to children or minors in a manner inconsistent with applicable law. We do not knowingly collect personal data from children or minors without an appropriate legal basis or any authorization required from a parent or legal guardian.

If we become aware that personal data relating to a child or minor has been collected inappropriately, we will take appropriate action in accordance with applicable legal requirements.

14. Third-Party Links and Services

Our website may contain links to third-party websites, tools, platforms, or services. We are not responsible for the privacy, security, or content practices of third parties. We encourage users to review the privacy policies of any third-party websites or services they access.

15. Changes to This Privacy Policy

We may amend or update this Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect changes in our practices, services, legal requirements, or operational needs. Any revised version will become effective when posted on this page, and the effective date will be updated accordingly.
